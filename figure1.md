## Quadrant Definitions

| Quadrant | Generality | Individuality | Example Systems |
|----------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Top-left | Low | High | Domain-expert agents with persistent user models, institutional memory |
| Top-right | High | High | **AGI (target)** — no current system |
| Bottom-left | Low | Low | Calculators, spell-checkers, narrow classifiers |
| Bottom-right | High | Low | GPT-4, Claude 3, Gemini Ultra — frontier LLMs |

## Key Claim Illustrated

All current AI systems cluster in the bottom half of this plane.
The X-axis-only view of AGI progress (moving right along the bottom)
mistakes movement toward the top-right quadrant for movement along the bottom.
Generality and individuality are **orthogonal** — scaling along X does not move you up Y.

## Figure Caption (draft)

**Figure 1 | The two-dimensional intelligence space.** 
Current AGI definitions evaluate systems along the X-axis of task generality — the breadth 
and depth of capability across domains. We propose a second axis, individuality, defined 
as the structural sedimentation of historical trajectory into a persistent, constraining 
identity. These axes are substantially orthogonal: frontier language models (bottom right) 
demonstrate high generality with negligible individuality. Genuine artificial general 
intelligence requires progress on both axes (top right). No current system approaches 
this quadrant.
